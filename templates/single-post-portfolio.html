The Portfolio Site was created to showcase both my design and technical skills for graduate school applications and professional opportunities. The project’s purpose was to present a comprehensive view of my design process, from ideation and wireframing to final implementation, while highlighting accessibility, usability, and visual design principles. The site also serves as a professional portfolio that communicates my personal design philosophy and approach to problem-solving.

Design process

Wireframes

Wireframes were developed to map out the information architecture, navigation, and layout hierarchy. They allowed for early visualization of page structure, user flows, and interactions, helping to identify potential usability issues before moving into high-fidelity design. Iterative feedback and revisions ensured the wireframes accurately represented the intended user experience.

Style guide / colour palette

A detailed style guide was created to maintain visual consistency across the site. This included typography choices, spacing, button styles, and a carefully selected color palette. Soft neutral backgrounds provided clarity and readability, while accent colors were used strategically to draw attention to key elements, calls-to-action, and interactive components.

Content Inventory

A content inventory was performed to audit all existing materials and plan the organization of text, images, and multimedia. This ensured each page had a clear purpose, avoided redundancy, and aligned with user needs. The inventory also helped prioritize content placement, establish hierarchy, and guide the design of responsive layouts for desktop and mobile.
